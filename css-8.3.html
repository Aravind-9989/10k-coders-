Aston Martin Lagonda Global Holdings plc is a British independent manufacturer of luxury sports cars and grand tourers. The company was founded in 1913 by Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford. The company was associated with expensive grand touring cars in the 1950s and 1960s. The brand further gained popularity with the use of a DB5 model in the popular 1964 James Bond film, Goldfinger. Aston Martin has over 150 car dealerships in over 50 countries on six continents, making them a global automobile brand. The company is headquartered in Gaydon, Warwickshire, England. One of Aston Martin''s recent cars was named after the 1950s Avro Vulcan bomber. Aston Martin has announced plans to turn itself into a global luxury brand, and is increasingly branching out into projects including speed boats, bicycles, clothing and real estate development
